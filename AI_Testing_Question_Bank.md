# AI Testing Question Bank

**Design Journey Tech Suite | Working Draft | Internal Only**

---

## What the AI Needs To Be Able To Do

- **Tag retrieval** — Return the actual tags that have been added to a given area
- **Plain-language synthesis** — Summarize tags and any additional written information into a coherent, natural-language answer that doesn't just list tag names
- **Cross-component awareness** — Retrieve and compare data across two or more components to identify overlap, gaps, and differences
- **Conditional reasoning / filtering** — Answer questions that require filtering components by a specific attribute
- **Rollup / aggregation** — Aggregate data across all components to answer school-wide and executive-level questions

---

## Data Sources

The AI reads from and should be able to ground answers in the following types of data:

- **Tags** — Outcomes, LEAPs, design principles, learner attributes, adult roles, and design element choice blocks (e.g. time block formats, pedagogical approaches, curriculum types)
- **Key markers** — Items flagged as "key" or "primary" across outcomes, grade bands, learner attributes, and adult roles
- **Priority levels** — High, Medium, or Low designations on outcomes and LEAPs
- **Written notes and descriptions** — Free-text notes attached to individual tags and selections
- **Plain-language text fields** — Descriptions, synthesis notes, and narrative explanations provided by users
- **Health scores and evaluations** — Dimension-level and measure-level scores, actor assessments, and weighted rollups
- **Conditions** — Headwinds and tailwinds tagged with stakeholder groups and the five C categories (Conviction, Capacity, Clarity, Culture, Coalition)
- **Chart data** — Student demographics, test scores, public academic profile, community reviews
- **Uploaded documents** — User-uploaded files attached to the school or components

## Section 1 — Component-Level Questions

Questions about a single Center Component, Ring Component, or Subcomponent. Each component answers independently.

### 1A. Journey & Overview (Center) / Snapshot (Ring & Subcomponent)

**Center Component — Journey & Overview**

<u>Mission & Context</u>

1) What is the school's mission?
2) Who are the key stakeholders for this school?
3) Describe the community context for this school.
4) Summarize the history of change efforts at this school.
5) What policy considerations affect this school?

<u>Student Demographics & Enrollment</u>

6) What do the student demographics look like for this school?
7) What is the racial and ethnic composition of the student body?

<u>Public Academic Profile</u>

8) What do the test scores show for this school?
9) How do low-income students perform compared to the overall student population?
10) What does the data show for students with disabilities?
11) What does the college prep data look like for this school?
12) Are there equity gaps in academic performance across student subgroups?
13) How does race and ethnicity correlate with academic performance at this school?

<u>Community Reviews</u>

14) What are the community reviews saying about this school?
15) How do families and staff rate the school, and where are the biggest gaps?

**Ring Component & Subcomponent — Snapshot**

16) What type of experience is [component] — learner or adult?
17) Give me an overview of [component].
18) What grade bands does [component] serve?
19) Which grade bands are marked as key for [component]?
20) How are students selected for [component]? Is participation required, optional, or targeted?
21) How many students and classrooms does [component] serve?
22) Which adults are involved in [component] and who specifically?
23) What is the time model for [component]? How long and how often does it meet?
24) When specifically does [component] meet — what days and times?

### 1C. Status & Health

#### Core Dimensions

Applies to the five core health dimensions: Design, Implementation, Experience, Learning & Advancement, and Wellbeing & Conduct. Each follows a similar structure of dimension, subdimension, measure, and actor evaluation.

83. How is [component] performing on [dimension, e.g. design]?
84. Tell me about [subdimension, e.g. coherence of design choices] within the [dimension] dimension for [component].
85. What does [specific measure] show for [component]?
86. What does [actor]'s evaluation say about [component]?
87. Summarize all of [actor]'s measures for [component].
88. How is [component] performing on learning and advancement outcomes?
89. How is [component] performing on wellbeing and conduct outcomes?
90. What is the experience score for [component], and how does it break down across LEAPs?
91. What are the overall strengths and weaknesses of [component]?
92. Give me the overall strengths and weaknesses of [component] across all health dimensions.

#### Conditions

Conditions are structured differently from the core dimensions — they are discrete headwind/tailwind events tagged with stakeholder groups and one of the five C categories (Conviction, Capacity, Clarity, Culture, Coalition).

93. What conditions are affecting [component]?
94. What conditions affect [stakeholder group, e.g. families] for [component]?
95. What are the [C category, e.g. clarity] conditions impacting [component]?
96. What are the [C category, e.g. clarity] headwinds for [component]?
97. What are the tailwinds supporting [component]?
98. Summarize the conditions landscape for [component] — what's helping and what's hurting?

## Section 2 — Blueprint-Level Questions

### 2A. Rollup / Structural

Questions that aggregate factual information across all components and subcomponents in a blueprint.

99. Which components serve [specific grade band, e.g. 9th grade]?
100. Which components target [specific outcome, e.g. "Identity & purpose"]?
101. How many components leverage [adult role, e.g. educators]?
102. Which LEAPs are most commonly prioritized across components?
103. Tell me which components leverage [curriculum, e.g. Eureka Math].

### 2B. Evaluation / Executive Synthesis

Questions that require the AI to assess, prioritize, and synthesize across the blueprint.

104. What are the most important outcomes for this school?
105. Which targeted outcomes are underrepresented across the blueprint?
106. Which primary outcomes that we target for the whole school are underrepresented across the blueprint?
107. How is the school doing on [C category, e.g. clarity]?
108. What are the most impactful conditions across all components impacting [C category, e.g. clarity]?
109. What are the most impactful conditions across [stakeholder group] impacting [C category]?
110. Which components are underperforming the most for [dimension, e.g. learning and advancement]?
111. Where are we underperforming the most across the school?

## Section 3 — Comparison Questions

Synthesis-oriented side-by-side comparison between two named Ring Components or two Blueprints. The AI should prioritize the most significant differences rather than producing exhaustive diffs.

**Component-to-Component**

112. What are the 3 most significant differences between [Ring A] and [Ring B]?
113. What do [Ring A] and [Ring B] have in common?
114. What are the key differences in outcomes between [Ring A] and [Ring B]?
115. How do the outcome priorities differ between [Ring A] and [Ring B]?
116. Compare the learner profiles of [Ring A] and [Ring B].
117. How do the adult roles differ between [Ring A] and [Ring B]?
118. Which component has stronger health scores, [Ring A] or [Ring B], and why?
119. Compare the conditions affecting [Ring A] vs [Ring B].

**Blueprint-to-Blueprint**

120. What are the key differences in outcomes between [School A] and [School B]?
121. How do the priorities of [School A] differ from [School B]?
122. Are there priorities that [School A] has that [School B] does not, and vice versa?
123. Compare the overall health of [School A] and [School B] — where is each stronger?
