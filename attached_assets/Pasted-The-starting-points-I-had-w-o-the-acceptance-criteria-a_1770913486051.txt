The starting points I had (w/o the acceptance criteria) are here

Ring Component Snapshot (“What is this?”)

As a school leader or design partner,

I want a clear, concise snapshot of a Ring Component

So that I can quickly understand what this component is, its purpose, and its key characteristics without drilling into detailed working spaces.

User Needs: The snapshot should answer:
- What is this component?
- What’s its purpose?
- What are the most important defining attributes?
- How is it positioned in the school’s overall design?

It is not meant to be exhaustive — it’s an orientation layer. Although it should be able to link the user to deeper layers where they can make additional changes as needed

Ring Component DEsigned Experience  (“What do kids do here?” / “What learning looks like”)

As a school leader or design partner,

I want to clearly see and define what students actually experience in this component / subcomponent

So that I can understand the intended learning model and assess how it aligns with our aims and outcomes.

User Needs: Designed Experience must show:
- What students are doing (subcomponents and/or uploaded resources that give an overview of the experience
- What learning this is trying to produce for students (aims for learners)
- What practices shape the experience (instructional / program practices)
- What supporting resources enable it (curriculum materials, tools, spaces, schedules, etc.)

Ring Component Health (“How’s it going?”)

As a school leader or design partner,

I want to see the overall health and journey status of a Ring Component or Subcomponent in one place

So that I can understand performance (E/O), drivers of performance (design implementation, conditions) and where we are in redesign — so that I can get an understanding how the component is doing (over time) and dig in to get a deeper understanding

User Need: 
- Performance Health (E/O)
- Driver Strength (Design, Implementation, Conditions)
- Journey Status (Baseline, Early Stage, etc.)
